As a student-oriented team, we aim to provide an amiable and enjoyable living to our residents. Feel free to find us if you need any help!

作為一個以學生為中心的團隊，我們旨在為宿生提供一個和藹可親和愉快的舍堂生活。
請隨時找我們幫助！

"ABC" Motto

"ABC"格言

Hall life is a part of university life where students can live, learn and grow happily together. To develop hall spirit, CSK Hall has its "ABC" motto - "Affection, Bonding and Care".

舍堂生活可以說是大學生涯中的重要一環。學生不但可以在舍堂住宿，更可同時在舍堂中學習以及成長。陳瑞球堂的"ABC"格言可以幫助宿生們了解本舍堂的精神。

Affection

Affection．舍堂情意

Students are encouraged to develop a sense of affection for each other through experiencing satisfactory, on-going relationships with their hall-mates. CSK Hall provides residents with a family atmosphere to form a solid foundation for their study and social activities.

陳瑞球堂鼓勵宿生互相建立持續的關係，享受彼此之間的情意。我們提供了一個充滿家庭氣氛的環境，深信這是宿生在這裡學習和社交的堅實基礎。

Bonding

Bonding．關係結連

This is the development of relationships based on mutual respect and acceptance of differences. We support all non-local students in our Hall as a way to embrace diversity. We encourage residents to integrate and participate in all Hall activities so as to build up multi-cultural friendships.

本著「互相尊重」、「求同存異」的精神，陳瑞球堂支援舍堂內的非本地學生融入舍堂生活之中。我們鼓勵不同背景的宿生一同參與舍堂活動，以建立多元文化的友誼。

Care

Care．宿生關懷

This is our care for the needs, well-being, and academic performance of our residents. We support each and every resident to achieve optimum academic performance, and we show our care by distributing fruits and drinks during the revision weeks. Other activities like study tours, "scholarship trips", and mental health talks will also be arranged to enhance broad-based learning.

陳瑞球堂關顧宿生的需要、福利以及學業成就。我們支援每一位同學取得最佳的學業成就。於考試「溫習週」，我們會派發水果及飲品予宿生，以鼓勵他們溫習。我們舉辦過的遊學團、「獎學金團」及精神健康講座等亦有助宿生去擴闊學習視野。


All these aspects in CSK Hall help our residents to take every opportunity to benefit from, and enjoy their university education to its fullest.




陳瑞球堂致力向宿生們提供各種機會，讓宿生能享受大學生涯的豐富之處。



About

The story of Hall 5, Chan Sui Kau Hall.

關於我們

第五座．陳瑞球堂的故事


  • 2000-2004

    Our Bright Beginnings

    2000年 至 2004年

    我們的起點

    In 2000, City University of Hong Kong launched the student resident project. Phase 1 development was completed in 2002, while Hall 5 was officially opened in Sep 2004, phase 2 of the project.

    香港城市大學於2000年開展學生宿舍興建計劃。
    該發展工程項目分四期進行。第一期的宿舍於2002年完成。而陳瑞球堂則在第二期發展時興建，於2004年9月份正式啓用。



  • April 2006

    Naming Ceremony of the Chan Sui Kau Hall (Hall 5)

    2006年 4月

    陳瑞球堂命名典禮 (第五座)

    Thanks to our donor Dr. Chan's donation, Hall 5 is named officially as "Chan Sui Kau Hall" since 12th April 2006.

    為感謝陳瑞球博士的捐資贊助，學生宿舍第五座於2006年4月12日正式命名為「陳瑞球堂」。




  • 2009

    2009年

Reminder of Hall Rules


Hope you are settling well at CSK Hall, are meeting friends and making new connections through various orientations, hall and inter-hall activities.

We recognise that the residence community is comprised of persons representing diversity and that every individual in the community, as part of this diversity, should be valued. We aspire to support the University core values of fostering civil dialogue and cultivating mutual respect by committing ourselves to ensure we all enjoy a safe and secure hall environment for all.

In achieving the above objectives, please be reminded that the "visiting hours" of all halls are from 8:00am to 12:00 midnight and no visitors (including residents from other halls) can stay in the hall outside these hours.

The period from 11:00pm to 7:00am is regarded as the quiet hours of the Student Residence. Please remember that noise travels from your room to your neighbors, or across the floors in the halls as well as to the neighboring estate, creating noise nuisance and disturbance to others.

During term time, Multi-function Hall B with wireless LAN has Multi-Leisure Time from 11:00pm to 2:00am. It is open for student residents to drop in for social interaction, card and board games. You may consider meeting your visitors or friends there after the hall visiting hours.

The Hall Management team, and our security guards are authorized to conduct identity checking and disperse gatherings that generate noise and other nuisance. We aspire every member of the hall community to foster community citizenship and responsibility. Any malpractice and violation of Hall rules may render residents liable to disciplinary procedures promulgated by the Hall Management and the University. Disciplinary actions by the Hall Management include (but not limited to): (a) verbal warning; (b) written warning that may be sent to the residents’ academic departments as the Hall Management thinks fit; (c) community services to the Hall; and (d) suspension of hall stay for a maximum of 8 weeks.

Our resident tutors and myself are look forward to getting to know our residents more and are available to help with the transition to CSK Hall. If you have any questions about anything related to residence life, feel free to check out the rest of CSK website, or FB, or email me directly: esther.chow.ss@cityu.edu.hk. Thank you, and see you around.

Esther
